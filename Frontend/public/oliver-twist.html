Charles Dickens

Oliver Twist

Dickens’ Victorian classic follows orphan Oliver as he escapes the brutality of a workhouse and steps into London’s dangerous underworld.

Oliver meets kind figures like Mr. Brownlow and Nancy, but also the manipulative Fagin and the cruel Bill Sikes, who pull him into theft and fear.

Story Highlights

- Oliver’s simple plea for more food gives the story its famous opening, showing the harsh conditions faced by children in Victorian England.

- The novel balances hope and hardship, as Oliver eventually finds safety with Mr. Brownlow while exposing the greedy and corrupt systems around him.

- Nancy’s courage and tragic choices reveal the novel’s deeper themes of compassion, sacrifice, and justice.
